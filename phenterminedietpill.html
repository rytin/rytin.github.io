Effects on weight reduction and safety of short-term phentermine administration in Korean obese people.

Yonsei Med J. 2006. Department of Family Medicine, Yonsei University College of Medicine, Seodaemun-gu, Seoul, Korea.

Phentermine diet pill, an appetite suppressant, has been widely used in Korea since 2004. The aim of this study doen at Yonsei University College of Medicine in Seoul, Korea was to determine the effect of phentermine diet pill on weight reduction. Obese subjects received phentermine HCl 37.5 mg or placebo once daily with behavioral therapy for obesity. Mean decrease of both body weight and waist circumference in phentermine diet pill treated subjects were significantly greater than that of placebo group. Significant number of subjects in phentermine diet pill group accomplished weight reduction of 5% or greater from the baseline and 10% or more. There were no significant differences in systolic and diastolic blood pressure between the groups. Dry mouth and insomnia were the only statistically significant adverse events that occurred more frequently in phentermine diet pill group. Most side effects of phentermine were mild to moderate in intensity.
